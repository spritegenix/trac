Cyber Security Awareness Training

Because Security Starts with People

You can invest in the best technology—but all it takes is one employee clicking the wrong link for everything to unravel.

TRAP (Threat ResQ Awareness Program) is our instructor-led cybersecurity awareness training designed to educate employees on real-world threats and help them build lasting security habits. Whether delivered in-person or virtually, TRAP helps turn your team into a smart, cyber-aware workforce that knows how to recognize and respond to threats.

The Human Element in Cyber Attacks

Did you know that over 90% of successful cyberattacks begin with a phishing email? Attackers don’t always break in—they log in, using credentials unknowingly handed over by employees.

 

And yet, this is also where you have the greatest opportunity to build resilience. According to studies, security awareness training can reduce phishing click rates by up to 75%—especially when paired with regular testing and reinforcement.

That’s the heart of what TRAP does:

Empowering your people to be alert, confident, and ready to act when something feels off.

Why TRAP Works

We believe cybersecurity training shouldn't be boring or overly technical. That’s why TRAP is built to be:

Relatable
Real stories, real examples, real threats.
Interactive
Live Q&A, demos, and audience engagement.
Flexible
Delivered online or on-site, tailored to your industry.
Practical
Focused on what employees need to know and do every day.

From new hires to leadership, TRAP helps everyone understand the role they play in protecting your organization.

What Your Employees Will Learn

TRAP goes beyond "don’t click suspicious links" and actually helps employees think like attackers—so they can stay one step ahead.

Key topics include:

  • Spotting phishing and social engineering attempts.
  • Creating strong passwords and using MFA .
  • Safe browsing and secure file sharing.
  • Identifying suspicious activity and reporting it early .
  • Protecting sensitive data inside and outside the workplace .

The content is regularly updated to reflect the evolving threat landscape, so your people stay prepared for what’s actually out there.

Training That Sticks

A typical TRAP session runs for 90–120 minutes, packed with real-world examples, interactive segments, and expert insights. There’s no fear-mongering or technical overload—just relatable, straightforward guidance from professionals who’ve seen these threats firsthand.

 

After the training, you’ll have the option to run a phishing simulation to test how well your team absorbed the material. It’s a safe, controlled way to spot risk areas and reinforce learning where it matters most.

 

And yes—it works:

Organizations that combine awareness training with phishing simulations report up to 50% fewer security incidents within just a few months.

Create a Security-First Culture

TRAP isn’t about a one-time workshop—it’s about changing how your people think about security. When your employees understand why something matters, they’re far more likely to do it.

Over time, that shift in mindset can reduce incidents, lower your risk, and strengthen your overall security posture.
